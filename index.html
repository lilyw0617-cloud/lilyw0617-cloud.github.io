Technology to Learn Korean Language & Culture

Group members: Wong Ming Lai · Yu Ying Ling

Learning Challenges / Problem Statement

  • Hangul is easy to read, but pronunciation is very difficult to master (e.g., batchim, tense/lax consonants)
  • Memorizing words without cultural context makes them easy to forget
  • Lack of interaction leads to shallow understanding of Korean culture (K-pop, K-dramas, food, etiquette)
  • Traditional learning methods feel boring and lack immediate feedback

Target users: Absolute beginners or elementary learners who want to learn Korean and experience the culture in a fun, technology-driven way

Our Solution — K-Culture Connect

A hybrid learning tool combining AI, QR codes, and a simple web prototype

  • AI chat companion for conversation practice and pronunciation correction
  • Scan QR codes on real objects or images → instant Korean vocabulary and cultural explanations
  • Cultural theme units (Korean food, hanok houses, K-drama expressions)
  • AI-generated personalized vocabulary and pronunciation exercises
“Scan, Chat, Learn — Immerse Yourself in Korean Culture”

IT Innovation (1) — How AI Supports Korean Learning

Using AI tools (similar to ChatGPT) as a 24/7 learning companion:

  • Real-time role-play: ordering food, asking directions, self-introductions
  • Pronunciation feedback: identifies batchim errors and tone issues
  • Generates culturally relevant example sentences (e.g., K-drama style dialogue)

Benefits: Available anytime, low-pressure practice, fully personalized learning

IT Innovation (2) — QR Codes & IoT

Scan everyday items, posters, or screenshots to unlock Korean language and culture content instantly.

  • Example: Scan a kimchi photo → “김치” pronunciation, making video, etiquette notes
  • Example: Scan a hanok image → “한옥” vocabulary and historical explanation
  • Turns ordinary objects into interactive learning triggers
